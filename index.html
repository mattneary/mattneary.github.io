Call me Matt...

I Make Mobile and Web Apps

I made my first app in 2011, an app and webservice for copy-and-paste storage of text, images, and links named CloudBoard. Then I started making education tools like Curves and Bonds. Since then, I have made various iOS and Android apps for clients, such as a social network and a platform for trade organizations.

In 2010, I made a game website, OmniGame. At one point it was attaining 10K daily views. I've also made various tools for the web, such as a drag-and-drop file transferrer, SMS messaging from the web, or a handwriting chat client.

I Really Like Languages

After exploring a multitude of languages from various paradigms, I decided that I would like to design my own. I began the process by creating a Lambda Calculus Interpreter, and then moved on to make my own Lisp, Lampas. Next I made a JavaScript dialect called Schript that is a bit like Lisp. More recently, I wrote a book titled Tarpits & Abstraction on interpretation, compilation, and related ideas.

I Love Open-Source

I Open-Source all of my projects, either as part of Lambda Labs, on my personal Github Page, or on my team's Github page. These projects include lots of tools: Quizzical is an API test-suite, zTest does statistical performance comparison, TeXode helps in type-setting books, and Edifice.js and FlightControl help in the building of server apps.

Additionally, I publish more theoretical endeavors, like AnInterpreter or Typical, a formal type-system for JavaScript. I contribute heavily to the projects of others, such as Lemonad, Birbal, and jquery-countdown. I ported Binary.js to iOS as well.

I Write About My Projects

I like doing formal research, perhaps in The Derivation of Rubik's Cube Methodoloy, Formal Verification of a Type-System, or Proving an Interpreter Correct. I write essays when I get the chance, and maintain Lingua Lambda; my book is quite similar to this publication.
